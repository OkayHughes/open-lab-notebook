we find that although we theoretically believe that we can set the tracer tendency c˙(t0)\dot{c}(t_0)c˙(t0​), the physics update is actually a first order explicit scheme, and so we have control over Δc,\Delta c, Δc, where c(t0+Δt)=c(t0)+ΔcΔtΔt. c(t_0 + \Delta t) = c(t_0) + \frac{\Delta c}{\Delta t} \Delta t.c(t0​+Δt)=c(t0​)+ΔtΔc​Δt. Note that we know the timestep Δt\Delta t Δt so we can cancel it automatically.

Note that the Δt\Delta t Δt given here is the Δtphys \Delta t_{\textrm{phys}} Δtphys​ using the notation of this presentation

I only really understand the time substepping scheme for SE, but as far as I can tell the of the three options laid out in this article tracer quantity will be added to the state in its entirety at the physics time step unless you force it to do "dribbling" which would be a wild choice for a non-thermodynamic tracer.

If the addition is happening all at once at the time of the physics update, then for the purposes of conservation all we care about is that we add a total amount of tracer mass to the grid point at this sudden update.

To make this fully concrete, suppose we take our state before the physics update is called: {T,U,V,P,cm}before physics\{T, U, V, P, c_m\}_{\textrm{before physics}}{T,U,V,P,cm​}before physics​. This state represents the diagnostic state of the atmosphere at the current time step after the previous dynamics and physics are finished. If what we care about is the total mass added to the atmosphere over the injection period, then all we are about is that from the time of the last physics update to this physics update we have added Δρm\Delta \rho_mΔρm​ of this tracer to the atmosphere.

As such, the discrete equation we need to satisfy is given {T,U,V,P,cm}before physics\{T, U, V, P, c_m\}_{\textrm{before physics}}{T,U,V,P,cm​}before physics​ we want that

ρm(ti,after injection)−ρm(ti,before injection)=Δρm,analytic≡∫ti−1tiρm,analytic(t) dt \rho_m(t_{i, \textrm{after injection}}) - \rho_m(t_{i, \textrm{before injection}}) = \Delta \rho_{m, \textrm{analytic}} \equiv \int_{t_{i-1}}^{t_{i}} \rho_{m, \textrm{analytic}}(t) \, \mathrm{d}tρm​(ti,after injection​)−ρm​(ti,before injection​)=Δρm,analytic​≡∫ti−1​ti​​ρm,analytic​(t)dt

where the sudden injection that is happening at t+Δtphyst + \Delta t_\mathrm{\mathrm{phys}}t+Δtphys​ is compensating for the injection over the last time step neglecting dynamics.

As such, we can calculate ρm(ti,after injection)=ρair(ti)⋅cm(ti,after injection) \rho_m(t_{i, \textrm{after injection}}) = \rho_{\mathrm{air}}(t_i) \cdot c_m(t_{i, \textrm{after injection}}) ρm​(ti,after injection​)=ρair​(ti​)⋅cm​(ti,after injection​)

and ρm(ti,before injection)=ρair(ti)⋅cm(ti,before injection) \rho_m(t_{i, \textrm{before injection}}) = \rho_{\mathrm{air}}(t_i) \cdot c_m(t_{i, \textrm{before injection}}) ρm​(ti,before injection​)=ρair​(ti​)⋅cm​(ti,before injection​)

And we can rearrange to find

ρair(ti)⋅cm(ti,after injection)−ρair(ti)⋅cm(ti,before injection)=Δρm,analytic \rho_{\mathrm{air}}(t_i) \cdot c_m(t_{i, \textrm{after injection}}) - \rho_{\mathrm{air}}(t_i) \cdot c_m(t_{i, \textrm{before injection}}) = \Delta \rho_{m, \textrm{analytic}}ρair​(ti​)⋅cm​(ti,after injection​)−ρair​(ti​)⋅cm​(ti,before injection​)=Δρm,analytic​ ρair(ti)⋅(cm(ti,before injection)+ΔtΔcmΔt)−ρair(ti)⋅cm(ti,before injection)=Δρm,analytic \rho_{\mathrm{air}}(t_i) \cdot \left(c_m(t_{i, \textrm{before injection}}) + \Delta t \frac{\Delta c_m}{\Delta t}\right) - \rho_{\mathrm{air}}(t_i) \cdot c_m(t_{i, \textrm{before injection}}) = \Delta \rho_{m, \textrm{analytic}}ρair​(ti​)⋅(cm​(ti,before injection​)+ΔtΔtΔcm​​)−ρair​(ti​)⋅cm​(ti,before injection​)=Δρm,analytic​ Δcm=Δρm,analyticρair(ti) \Delta c_m = \frac{\Delta \rho_{m, \textrm{analytic}}}{\rho_{\mathrm{air}}(t_i)}Δcm​=ρair​(ti​)Δρm,analytic​​

The long and short of it is, this will precisely control the amount of mass added but will not spread tracer insertion over tracer advection timesteps. If you really want to have both, set the dynamics and the physics time step to be the same. In the HSW idealized setup the added time will be negligible.

To sum up the single trick that I used, because the code can be made to instantaneously update the tracer by a quantity before dynamics or tracer advection starts up again, we get to make a principled assumption that ρair \rho_{\mathrm{air}} ρair​ is constant during our update step, which is actually what the code is doing.

The reason I'm using the {T,U,V,P,cm}before physics\{T, U, V, P, c_m\}_{\textrm{before physics}}{T,U,V,P,cm​}before physics​ state is that sometimes these quantities are dribbled over the previous dynamics substepping. By doing the update right when the physics step begins, your update will be consistent with the diagnosed density at the beginning of the physics timestep.
