The mathematics of lamb waves

In the atmosphere there is a special kind of acoustic wave called a Lamb wave, which is a horizontally propagating sound wave with no vertical velocity propagation (w′w'w′ = 0). They are supported by a hydrostatic equation set. It has been observed that the amplitudes of the oscillations of a Lamb wave vary with height.
